Introduction

Gas vehicles contribute significantly to climate change, air pollution, and health problems. This website gathers research, case studies, statistics, and key vocabulary to support the debate on why gas vehicles should be banned in the future.

Reasons to Ban Gas Vehicles

  • Air pollution
  • High greenhouse gas emissions compared to EVs
  • Fossil fuel dependence
  • Methane leakage contributes to climate change
  • Noise pollution in urban areas
  • Limited fuel efficiency compared to EVs
  • Safety risks with high-pressure gas storage
  • Non-zero carbon footprint throughout lifecycle
  • Conflicts with global net zero emissions goals
  • Higher particulate emissions than modern EVs
  • Reduced air quality in enclosed spaces
  • Infrastructure cost for CNG/LPG stations
  • Market trend moving toward fully electric vehicles
  • Delayed transition to carbon-neutral transport
  • Risk of stranded assets in automotive industry

Evidence & Case Studies

  • China: EV charging reduced NO₂ and PM₂.₅ in 292 cities (Nature)
  • Wuhan study: EV adoption improved air quality (ScienceDirect)
  • California: Zero-emission vehicles reduced asthma emergency visits (ScienceDaily)
  • Chandigarh, India: EV boom led to CO₂ savings (~26x in 5 years) (Times of India)
  • Norway: Almost 90% of new cars electric in 2024 (Washington Post)
